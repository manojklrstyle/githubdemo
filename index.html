Space Insights

Space is incredibly vast; it takes over 8 minutes for light from the Sun to reach Earth.

Voyager 1, launched by NASA in 1977, continues to journey into interstellar space, providing invaluable insights into the outer reaches of our solar system. Jupiter, with its swirling storms and fascinating moons, stands as a testament to the dynamic forces shaping our celestial neighborhood.

Neutron stars are extremely dense; a tiny piece the size of a sugar cube would weigh about a billion tons on Earth.

The observable universe has around 2 trillion galaxies, each with millions or billions of stars.

Space Missions

Voyager 2

Voyager 2 is a NASA space probe launched on August 20, 1977, to explore the outer planets. It is the only spacecraft to visit Uranus and Neptune, sending back important data and images. Voyager 2 discovered volcanoes on Jupiter's moon Io, rings around Saturn, and provided detailed information about Uranus and Neptune. After finishing its main mission, it entered interstellar space on November 5, 2018, and still sends data from beyond our solar system

Chandrayaan 2

Chandrayaan-3 is a follow-up mission to Chandrayaan-2, aimed at demonstrating the ability to land safely and operate a rover on the Moon. It includes a lander and rover and will be launched by LVM3 from SDSC SHAR in Sriharikota. The propulsion module will carry the lander and rover to a 100 km lunar orbit. It also has a SHAPE payload to study Earth's spectral and polarimetric measurements from the Moon's orbit.
